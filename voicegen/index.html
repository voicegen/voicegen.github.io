Describing and Generating Voices with Text Prompt


Abstract

In this paper, we introduce an innovative method on generating voices for speech synthesis using text descriptions as prompt. Traditional Text-to-Speech (TTS) systems depend on reference audio for style control, resulting in reduced flexibility and user-friendliness. Consequently, recent research has focused on controlling speech synthesis style through text descriptions. However, existing text description-based TTS approaches face challenges such as requiring human experts to write descriptions and confronting the one-to-many problem due to limited fine-grained style information in the text. To address these issues, we (1) leverage ChatGPT to generate fluent descriptions centered on specific speech style keywords, and carefully design prompts with keyword placeholders to counteract ChatGPT's instability, which may produce excessive details or miss crucial keywords. Furthermore, we (2) employ a variational auto-encoder (VAE) to model the residual style information not captured by the text description. By incorporating a gradient reversal layer, the VAE module efficiently disentangles style information from both the text description and reference audio. Our experiments on a large-scale datasets with 44K hours of speech data show that our method outperforms previous works in style control accuracy. And ChatGPT-generated descriptions demonstrate marginally higher quality than human-authored ones. Thanks to the style disentanglement, our system is able to facilitate style generation by sampling from noise during inference. An online demo of our system is available for further exploration.
